# Session 03 — Presenting a Dashboard
**Date:** 2026-06-09  
**Scenario:** Presenting a sales dashboard to a Product Manager (Jamie) — Northwind dataset  

---

## Conversation

**Jamie:** Hey! I heard you finished the sales dashboard. Can you walk me through it?

**Me:** Hi Jamie! Yes, on the Northwind dataset you can see the key metrics: total sales of $1.27 million, 89 active customers, 831 total orders, and 2 inactive customers. There is also a product ranking by performance.

**Jamie:** Wow, that's a solid dashboard! So total sales are $1.27 million — is that across all years? And I see you have a ranking of products, which ones are the top performers?

**Me:** Yes, this is a dashboard across all years. The top product by performance is Camembert Pierrot. In another section you can also see one product with no sales.

**Jamie:** Interesting! And what about the employees — I can see there's something about orders and sales by employee. Who's the top performer there?

**Me:** Yes, employee Peacock is number one in the ranking — both by number of orders and total sales.

**Jamie:** Great insights! And I noticed there are two inactive customers — FISSA Fabrica and Paris Specialties. Do you know why they stopped buying?

**Me:** I would recommend offering them a targeted promotion to bring them back.

**Jamie:** That's a great recommendation! A targeted promotion could definitely bring them back. Really nice work on this dashboard — very clear and actionable. Thanks for walking me through it!
