# Session 07 — Handling Unknown Answers in Meetings
**Date:** 2026-06-13  
**Scenario:** Product meeting with Jamie — responding when you don't know the answer  
**Duration:** ~15 minutes  

---

## Conversation

**Jamie:** Hey! Quick question — do you know why the sales dropped in Q3 last year?

**Me:** We are investigating that cause.

**Jamie:** Great, and when do you think you'll have more information on that?

**Me:** I need to investigate that further.

**Jamie:** Perfect. Can you send me a summary of your findings?

**Me:** That's a great question. Let me look into that.

**Jamie:** Sounds good. When can I expect that from you?

**Me:** I'll send you an update by end of day.

**Jamie:** Excellent! Thanks for your quick response.

---

## Key phrases learned today
1. **"I need to investigate that further."** — When you need more time to analyze
2. **"That's a great question. Let me look into that."** — Professional way to say "I don't know but I'll find out"
3. **"I'll send you an update by end of day."** — For committing to deliverables

These three phrases let you sound professional when you don't have an immediate answer.
